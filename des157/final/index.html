Covid 19's Impact on Historically Excluded Communities

Since the beginning of the pandemic, we have seen more than 1.7 million deaths and over 5 million job losses for people due to Covid-19. But who are the ones who have been the most impacted? Scroll to learn more.

  • First apparent case reported by Orange County Health Care agency in california on Jan, 2020
  • World Health Organization categorizes Covid-19 as a global pandemic
  • United states becomes hardes hit country with more than 81,321 confirmed infections
  • Beginning of school closures and work closures; 10 million Americans out of work
  • Covid-19 deaths surpass 100,000
  • Black Americans and Latinos 3 times as likely to know someone who died of Covid-19 compared to white people, Native Americans 3.3 more likely to die
  • Increase of hate crimes against Asian Americans begin, with more than 2,500 reported cases
  • Due to most of essential workers being Black and Latino and inability to work remotely, unemployment rates tripled for Black(16.1%), Latinxs(16.7%), and Asians(14.3%), with Black women making up the highest unemployment rate.
  • In California, 22% of black people and 26% of latinos were jobless compared to 17% of white and Asians.
  • Black and Latinos were less likely to be covered by unemployment insurance
  • Despite being the hardest hit, Black and Latinos were underrepresented in vaccine trials, with mistrust playing a factor due to historical systemic racism in the medical field but disengagement of doctors width the communities most impacted being the most significant factor.
  • 22.6% of black renters, 19.5% of latino renters, and 19.4% reported being behind on rent, compared to 9.8% of whites. 9.7% of Black renters and 8.7% of latinxs renters reported likely to be evicted compared to 3.7% of Asians and 4.4% of white people.
    • Nationally, Black, Latinx, and Native American students were hit hardest from school closures, with test scores in math and reading being lower than whites.
    • As of May 25,2021, white people make up 62% of the vaccinations compared to 9% of black people, 14% latinxs, 6% asians, 1% Native American, and 1% Hawaiian or Pacific Islander
    • Nationally, 60,298 latinos and 73,462 black lives were lost, as they were 2x more likely than white people to die.
    • Despite USA covid-19 cases recorded at the lowest level in over a year, yet it is still affecting Black and brown communities disproportionately.
    • Globally, while the United States is administering 50 doses per 100 people, making it one of the countries higher vaccination rates along with other first world and white majority countries, countries with mostly black, brown and asian populations are still heavily unvaccinated and could take up to 2+ years to fully get vaccinated.
